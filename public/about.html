I didn’t plan to become a coach.

I planned to win.

I grew up with one goal — succeed at the highest level possible. I played college basketball. I graduated magna cum laude from NYU. I went to Wall Street. Then Silicon Valley. I was doing everything right by every external measure.

Then I attended a Tony Robbins event.

I walked in as someone who had built a successful life on the outside. I walked out knowing that the operating system underneath needed a complete rebuild. Within a year I had left my career to go work for Tony Robbins Companies as a national speaker and trainer.

For five years I stood on stages across America. Over 1,000 talks. More than 50,000 people. I watched founders, executives, and operators transform in real time — not because of information, but because something shifted underneath.

I also watched what happened to the ones who didn’t do the work. They kept performing. They kept achieving. And they kept feeling like something was missing — even when the numbers said otherwise.

That pattern became my life’s work.

I spent the next decade building a methodology — the Mood Method — around one core truth: your mood is your operating system. Your identity, your relationships, your relationship with money, your leadership — all of it runs through the quality of your internal state.

I am an ICF accredited coach, NLP trainer and hypnotist, and certified with Performance Coach University. I work with a small number of founders each year inside the SuperMood Standard — a 12-month private container designed to transform all three dimensions of a founder’s life: Peak Mind, Peak Body, and Peak Love.

This is the work I was built to do.
